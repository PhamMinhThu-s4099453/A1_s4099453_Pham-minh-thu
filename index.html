In this heat-haze shimmered light,

I ask the boundless sky: my future, will you ignite?"

My fists are carved by the dreams I've gripped,

And wings that ache from every fall.
I chase a spark no textbook ever held,

Where scars, like feathers, rise

and

are beheld.

Still, I rise; still, I fly.

Though thunder growls and rain strives to blacken the sky.

When time slows into a golden hush,

And the world holds its breath,

As I leap.

Even if I lose, if I crack, if I burn,

My soul will stand,

a diamond, stern.

 

Let their maps show no such path.

Even limits can become our wings to ascend .

I'll carry this imagination, My blazing heart,

And fly like a fierce phoenix, From embers, to zenith.
